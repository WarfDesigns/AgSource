What to expect in the first 90 days

Days 1–10

Listen & baseline assessment

We start with a farm visit and intake to understand goals, field history, and current practices.

  • Farm walk-through and operator interview
  • Review previous soil and yield data
  • Set short-term priorities together
Days 11–45

Field analysis & seasonal plan

Days 46–90

In-season support & checkpoints

Once the plan is in motion, we stay close with scouting, check-ins, and adjustments.

  • Harvest or post-harvest preparation

Frequently asked questions

Quick answers on timing, sampling, and what to expect before you request a farm visit.

How often should we sample soil?

Most operations sample every 2–4 years, with annual check-ins on high-priority fields or new rentals.

What’s the typical turnaround time?

Lab results are usually ready in 7–10 business days, and we review recommendations with you right away.

What does a farm visit include?

A field walk, equipment and input review, plus a sit-down to align on goals, budgets, and next steps.

When is the best season to start?

Fall and early spring are ideal for planning, but we support in-season scouting and mid-year adjustments.
